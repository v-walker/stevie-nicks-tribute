Stephanie "Stevie" Lynn Nicks

Beloved American Queen of Rock and Roll

Here is a timeline of Stevie's wondrous life:

  • 1948 - Nicks is born in Phoenix Arizona to Jess and Barbara Nicks.
  • 1963 - The Nicks family relocates to the suburbs of Los Angeles, California, where she enrolls at Arcadia High School.
  • 1964 - Stevie turns sixteeen. For her birthday, she receives a Goya guitar, which she uses to write her first song titled "I've Loved and I've Lost, and I'm Sad but Not Blue." During this time, she attends Arcadia High School and joins the band, The Changing Times.
  • ~1995-1966 - The Nicks family relocates to Palo Alto, California, where Stevie attends Menlo Atherton High, and meets future band-mate and romantic interest Lindsey Buckingham.
  • 1967 - Buckingham asks Nicks to replace the lead singer of his band, Fritz.
  • ~1967-1968 - Nicks and Buckingham attend San Jose State College, where Nicks studies speech communication. Nicks drops out of college in 1968 to pursue her music career.
  • 1968-1970 - Fritz opens for Jimi Hendrix and Janis Joplin.
  • 1972 - Fritz disbands, while Nicks and Buckingham continue to write together.
  • 1973 - Nicks and Buckingham secure a deal with Polydor Records and release the album Buckingham Nicks. The album was unsuccessful, and Polydor later dropped the duo. Nicks writes the songs "Rhiannon" and "Landslide."
  • 1974 - Buckingham and Nicks join Fleetwood Mac as "a package deal."
  • 1975 - The album Fleetwood Mac is released to world-wide critical acclaim. "Rhiannon" is voted one of the 500 Greatest Songs of All Time by Rolling Stone. Nicks ends her relationship with Buckingham later that year.
  • 1976 - Fleetwood Mac begins recording the album Rumors. Nicks contributes the song "Dreams" to this album, which later became the band's only Billboard Hot 100 number-one hit single. Nicks also writes, "Silver Springs," which does not make it onto Rumors but is later released as a B-side of the "Go Your Own Way" single.
  • 1977 - Rumors is the best-selling album of the year and remains the number one album on the American albums chart for 31 weeks.
  • 1978 - Rumors wins a Grammy Award for Album of the Year.
  • 1979 - Fleetwood Mack's third studio album, Tusk is released. Meanwhile, Nicks writes and records songs for a solo project.
  • 1981 - Nicks releases her first solo studio album, Bella Donna, reaching number one on the Billboard 200 chart.
  • 1982 - Nicks marries Kim Anderson, and they divorce three months later.
  • 1983 - Nicks releases her second solo album Wild Heart.
  • 1985 - Nicks releases Rock a Little and tours alongside Bob Dylan and Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers.
  • 1986 - Nicks checks in to the Betty Ford Center due to coccaine addiction.
  • 1987 - Fleetwood Mac releases the album Tango in the Night. Lindsey Buckingham leaves Fleetwood Mac due to creative differences and personal issues.
  • 1988 - Fleetwood Mac releases their Greatest Hits album.
  • 1989 - Nicks releases her fourth solo album, The Other Side of the Mirror.
  • 1990 - Fleetwood Mac releases the album Behind the Mask. Nicks leaves Fleetwood Mac after a dispute with Mick Fleetwood over the song "Landslide".
  • 1991 - Nicks releases Timespace: The Best of Stevie Nicks.
  • 1993 - Nicks goes through a 47 day detox from Klonopin (prescribed in increasingly stronger doses over the years following her rehab from coccaine) in a hospital after experiencing a fall and injury.
  • 1994 - Nicks releases the album Street Angel, which was poorly received.
  • 1996 - Nicks works with a voice coach to gain better control over her voice and protect it. Fleetwood Mac is reunited and goes on The Dance tour and releases a live CD titled The Dance, garnering several Grammy nominations.
  • 1998 - Fleetwood Mac is inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
  • 2001 - Nicks releases Trouble in Shangri-La.
  • 2003 - Fleetwood Mac releases the album Say You Will, and Nicks joins along with the band for their world tour.
  • 2007 - Reprise Records releases Crystal Visons - The Very Best of Stevie Nicks, debuting at number 21 on the Billboard 200 albums chart.
  • 2009 - Nicks releases the album, The Soundstage Sessions.
  • 2011 - Nicks releases In Your Dreams, debuting at number six on the Billboard 200 album chart.
  • 2014 - Nicks appears on season three of American Horror Story, Coven, playing a fictional version of herself as a "white witch."
  • 2014 - Nicks releases her eighth studio album, 24 Karat Gold: Songs from the Vault and begins a world tour with Fleetwood Mac.
  • 2019 - Nicks is elected to the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, becoming the first woman to be inducted twice.
